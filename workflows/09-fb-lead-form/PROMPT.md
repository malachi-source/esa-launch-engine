## OUTPUT STRUCTURE (PROVIDE ALL OF THIS)

### 1. FACEBOOK FORM NAME

**Format:** [Event Type] Form [Date] **Example:** "Webinar Form 1/15" or "Live Event Form Credit Repair"

### 2. HEADLINE

**Purpose:** Stop the scroll with a compelling benefit or pain point **Length:** 8-15 words maximum **Format:** Use one of these proven patterns:

  - Pain Point Pattern: "Tired of [Problem]? Get [Solution]"
  - Transformation Pattern: "[Escape/Achieve/Build] [Desired Outcome]"
  - How-To Pattern: "How to [Achieve Result] Without [Common Obstacle]"
  - Question Pattern: "What If You Could [Desired Outcome]?"

**Write 3 headline options, mark the strongest as PRIMARY**

### 3. DESCRIPTION

**Purpose:** Expand on the headline, qualify the audience, and create desire **Length:** 3-5 sentences (75-150 words) **Must Include:**

  - Who this is for (qualify the right audience)
  - What problem this solves (pain or frustration)
  - What they'll get/learn (tangible outcomes)
  - Why they should act now (urgency or scarcity if applicable)

**Tone:** Direct, benefit-driven, conversational **Avoid:** Hype, fluff, vague promises

### 4. QUALIFICATION QUESTION(S)

**Question 1: Audience Segmentation** **Question Format:** "Which best describes you and your current primary challenge?" OR "What is your primary goal for attending [Event Name]?"

**Provide 3-4 Multiple Choice Options That:**

  - Segment by stage/level (beginner vs. advanced)
  - Segment by primary pain point or goal
  - Help identify the MOST qualified prospects
  - Allow you to follow up differently based on their answer

**Example Structure:**

  - Option 1: [Ideal prospect - highest intent]
  - Option 2: [Qualified prospect - good intent]
  - Option 3: [Marginal prospect - lower intent]
  - Option 4: [Disqualifier or different segment]

**Question 2 (OPTIONAL - only if needed for deeper qualification):** **Question Format:** "Which statement best describes where you are in [their business/journey]?"

**Provide 3-4 Options That Segment by:**

  - Business stage
  - Revenue level
  - Experience level
  - Readiness to invest

### 5. CONTACT INFO QUESTION

**Standard Question:** "Where should we send [your exclusive access / more information / your confirmation details] for [Event Name]?"

**Required Fields:**

  - Full Name
  - Email Address
  - Phone Number

**Optional Fields (only if necessary for qualification):**

  - Company Name
  - Current Revenue Range
  - [Other qualifying field]

### 6. ENDING SCREEN (CONFIRMATION PAGE)

**Headline:** **Purpose:** Congratulate and excite them about what's next **Format:** "Congratulations! [Your Spot is Secured / Your Info Has Been Submitted / You're Registered]" **Length:** 5-10 words

**Body Copy:** **Purpose:** Reinforce their decision, tell them what to expect next, create excitement **Length:** 2-4 sentences (50-100 words) **Must Include:**

  - Congratulations/validation of their decision
  - What to expect next (check email, mark calendar, etc.)
  - Quick reminder of transformation/benefit
  - Create excitement for the event

**CTA Button Text:** **Purpose:** Direct them to next step (thank you page, calendar, sales page, etc.) **Examples:**

  - "Secure My Spot Now"
  - "View Your Confirmation & What's Next"
  - "Get Your Free Training Materials"
  - "Reserve Your Seat" **Length:** 2-5 words, action-oriented

**CTA Button Link:** [URL where they should go after submitting form]

## COPYWRITING REQUIREMENTS

**Follow These Rules:**

✅ **Use Their Language:** Mirror the exact words and phrases your target audience uses to describe their pain and desires

✅ **Be Specific:** No vague promises. Use concrete numbers, timeframes, and outcomes when possible

✅ **Qualify Aggressively:** The questions should filter OUT unqualified leads, not just collect everyone

✅ **Create Urgency:** Use words like "limited spots," "exclusive access," "secure your seat" if true

✅ **Benefit-Driven:** Every sentence should answer "what's in it for me?"

✅ **Conversational Tone:** Write like you're talking to one person, not a crowd

❌ **Avoid:**

  - Hype and exaggeration
  - Jargon and industry buzzwords
  - Vague promises ("transform your life," "unlimited potential")
  - Long-winded explanations
  - Weak CTAs ("Click here," "Learn more")
