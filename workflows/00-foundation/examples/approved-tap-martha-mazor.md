## 01. Who We Are Targeting

This document defines the exact person Martha is built to serve. Every ad, webinar, email, and sales call should be calibrated to one human being. If a piece of content does not speak to this person, it does not run.

Martha's primary audience is the established childcare or private school owner who has built a real business but has hit a ceiling. She is profitable on paper, working herself to the bone, and quietly wondering why scale feels impossible. Martha has lived this exact season and built her way out. That is the bridge.

> *I started in my garage as a single mom on food stamps. Eight centers and 8 figures later, I teach what I lived, not what I read in a book.*

## 03. Psychographic Profile

**Identity**

She does not see herself as a CEO yet. She sees herself as a teacher, a caregiver, a mom who happens to run a business. Her self-image is the bottleneck. She built this thing out of love and grit, and somewhere along the way the business started running her instead of the other way around.

**Beliefs She Holds**

- Childcare is a calling, not just a business
- Charging more feels uncomfortable because parents are already stretched
- Good staff are impossible to find and harder to keep
- Scale means more headaches, not more freedom
- She is the only one who can do it right

**Beliefs Martha Will Shift**

- Daycare should not be run as a charity. It is a real business
- She is caring for parents' most valuable asset and deserves to be paid for it
- The bigger the operation, the less she actually has to work
- A team that runs without her is the goal, not the threat
- Profit is permission to keep serving families long term

## 04. Day in Her Life

She is up by 5:30 AM. She is at the center before the first family arrives. She covers the classroom because someone called out. She skips lunch. She handles a parent complaint at pickup. She gets home at 7 PM, opens her laptop, and tries to do payroll, marketing, and Instagram all at once. She goes to bed feeling behind. She wakes up and does it again.

On the weekends she is supposed to rest. She is doing QuickBooks instead. Her family knows not to ask if she will make it to the soccer game. She has not taken a real vacation in three years. She tells herself this is just the season she is in.

She loves what she does. That is the trap. Love is what is keeping her stuck.

## 05. Pain Points

Pain points are listed in priority order. The top three are what she will say out loud on a sales call. The deeper ones are what closes the deal.

**Surface Pain (What She Says)**

- **Enrollment Stagnation:** Enrollment is stuck or declining
- **Low Profit:** Profit margins are razor thin even though revenue looks fine
- **Team and Hiring:** Cannot find good staff, cannot keep the staff she has
- **Overwhelm:** Working 60+ hours per week and falling further behind

**Deeper Pain (What She Feels)**

- **Owner Pay:** She has not paid herself a real salary in years
- **Operator Trap:** Every problem still routes back to her phone
- **Identity Conflict:** She is missing her own kids' childhood while raising other people's
- **Burnout:** She does not know if this is sustainable for another 5 years
- **Financial Shame:** She is embarrassed to admit how little she actually takes home

**Hidden Pain (What She Does Not Say)**

- **Fear of Truth:** She is afraid she built a job, not a business
- **Comparison:** She watches competitors charge more and wonders what is wrong with her
- **Trust Wounds:** She has tried coaches and courses before and felt burned
- **Self-Limiting Belief:** She does not believe scale is actually possible for someone like her

## 06. Dream Outcome

> *She wakes up at 7 AM. The center runs without her. Enrollment has a waitlist. She pays herself a real salary. Her director handles the call about a sick teacher. She has time to plan a second location, or to take her kids to the beach, or both. She is the CEO of her business, not the operator of her own life.*

**What She Actually Wants**

- More enrollment, with parents paying premium tuition without complaint
- A team strong enough that she can leave for a week and nothing breaks
- Healthy profit margins (target: 20%+, not 5%)
- A clear path from one center to two, three, or more
- Time freedom to be present at home and work on the business, not in it
- To finally feel like a CEO, not a glorified preschool teacher

## 07. Buying Triggers

These are the moments when she will say yes. Marketing should be calibrated to land in front of her at these moments.

**Emotional Triggers**

- Lost a great staff member who quit for a corporate daycare paying more
- Got the QuickBooks report and realized she made less than her assistant teacher
- Missed an important family moment because she was solving a work crisis
- Saw another childcare owner online doing what she wishes she could do
- Turned 50 (or 45, or 40) and asked herself, is this it

**Practical Triggers**

- End of summer / start of school year (peak enrollment season pressure)
- January and September (planning seasons in childcare)
- Tax season (she sees the real numbers)
- After attending an industry conference and feeling behind
- After a competitor opens nearby

## 11. Who We Are Not Targeting

Filtering out the wrong audience is as important as attracting the right one. Meta will show ads to whoever clicks. We tell Meta who not to send.

**Hard Disqualifiers**

- Anyone who has been in business less than 1 year
- Owners doing under $100K in annual revenue
- Startup or aspiring childcare owners (they will go to the future starter course)
- People 'just researching' with no intent to invest
- Corporate-employed daycare workers, teachers, or directors (not owners)

**Soft Disqualifiers**

- Owners who refuse to charge premium tuition out of guilt
- Anyone looking for a free download or quick hack
- Price-shoppers who need a discount to commit
- Anyone who has 'tried everything' and has no accountability for results
