## 01. Executive Summary

The Credit Repair Business Bootcamp serves credit repair entrepreneurs who have proven they can generate revenue but feel stuck between ten and fifteen thousand dollars in monthly income. Our primary objective is to fill two paid live events (LA on June 6 and NYC on June 19) with a combined target of 400 to 600 qualified attendees, then convert a meaningful share into the $7,500 six-month backend coaching program. The avatar described in this document is the filter every ad creative, funnel page, nurture message, and AI agent conversation should be measured against.

## 02. Primary Avatar: The Stuck Operator

**Marcus, The Stuck Operator**

A credit repair business owner who has been running for one to four years, closes real clients every month, and has hit a revenue ceiling between $10,000 and $15,000 per month. He knows his craft, but he is trading hours for dollars, doing most of the work himself, and suspects that the next level requires systems, automation, and leverage rather than more hustle. He is tired of flashy gurus but genuinely wants to build a compliant, scalable business.

**Core Demographics**

| Attribute | Detail |
| :-- | :-- |
| Age Range | 30 to 48 (core), 28 to 55 (outer band) |
| Gender Split | Approximately 55% male, 45% female |
| Income | Personal draw of $60,000 to $140,000 from a business doing $120,000 to $250,000 per year |
| Education | High school through Bachelor's. Often self-taught or bootcamp-educated |
| Location (LA event) | Southern California, Nevada, Arizona, plus warm markets in TX and AZ |
| Location (NYC event) | NY, NJ, CT, PA, MD, MA, and surrounding Tri-State metros |
| Family Status | Often married with one or two children, financially responsible for household |
| Business Stage | 12 to 48 months in operation, 10 to 60 active clients |

## 03. Pain Points vs. Desired Outcomes

**What Keeps Them Up at Night**

- Stuck at $10K to $15K per month with no clear path up
- Doing disputes, sales, billing, and support alone
- Inconsistent lead flow and feast or famine cycles
- Fear of CFPB, FTC, and state compliance issues
- No real CRM, losing leads in texts, DMs, and spreadsheets
- Saturated market where everyone sounds the same
- Burned by previous courses that did not deliver
- Cannot confidently hire, delegate, or build a team

**What They Actually Want**

- Predictable path from $15K to $50K, $60K, then $100K months
- Automated client acquisition that runs without them
- A compliant, bulletproof business they can sleep on
- AI driven tools like Violation Scan Pro doing the heavy lifting
- Real systems, SOPs, and a trainable team
- Authority and recognition in the credit repair space
- Recurring monthly revenue that is not dependent on churn
- Proof that it is possible, not just promises

## 04. Psychographics and Mindset

**Values They Hold**

- Compliance and transparency over shortcuts
- Long term wealth building, not quick cash
- Family, freedom, and legacy
- Faith based or community oriented in many cases
- Investing in education and mentorship

**Beliefs They Already Hold**

- The industry is saturated with fakes
- Systems beat hustle at a certain level
- Most coaches over promise and under deliver
- AI and automation are the future of the space
- A real business needs a real team

> **Positioning insight:** Because this avatar has usually been burned before, our messaging must over index on proof, compliance, and specificity. Vague six figure month language will attract tire kickers. Concrete numbers, frameworks, and named tools (Violation Scan Pro, GoHighLevel, A2P compliance, AI reactivation) build trust fastest.

## 05. Awareness and Sophistication

| Dimension | Profile |
| :-- | :-- |
| Market Awareness | Problem aware and solution aware. They know they need systems, automation, and mentorship. Most are still product unaware of what a proper bootcamp experience delivers. |
| Sophistication | Stage 4 to 5. They have seen generic ads (make $10k per month), cheap courses, and free webinars. Hooks must lead with mechanism, not result. |
| Skepticism Level | High. Many have bought at least one program that failed them. They trust specificity, real case studies, and operators who are clearly still in the trenches. |
| Buying Trigger | A recent plateau month, a compliance scare, losing a big client, or watching a peer scale past them. |

## 06. Secondary Avatar: The Launch Ready Starter

**Tasha, The Launch Ready Starter**

An aspiring credit repair entrepreneur who is still in a W2 job or running a related service business (real estate, insurance, mortgage, tax prep). She has researched the industry, knows there is real money here, and is looking for a legitimate, compliant way to build a credit repair business from zero without getting burned by a guru. She has savings, high intent, and is often the household decision maker.

**Core Demographics**

| Attribute | Detail |
| :-- | :-- |
| Age Range | 28 to 50 |
| Gender Split | Approximately 60% female, 40% male |
| Income | $55,000 to $110,000 W2 or 1099 |
| Current Role | Loan officer, real estate agent, insurance rep, financial services W2, military, or HR |
| Motivation | Replace or supplement W2 income with a real, compliant business |

> **Split messaging note:** Both avatars can buy tickets, but they convert to backend coaching at different rates and for different reasons. Marcus buys to scale. Tasha buys to launch. Creative and nurture sequences should be segmented so the AI agent can route each lead into the right narrative.

## 07. Event Specific Audience Targeting

**Los Angeles — June 6, 2026**

- **Geo radius:** 60 miles from venue, plus Las Vegas, Phoenix, and San Diego as secondary drive markets.
- **Cultural notes:** Heavy Latino and Black entrepreneur base. Spanish language creative should be tested as a secondary audience.
- **Industry density:** High concentration of real estate and mortgage professionals who already cross sell credit repair.
- **Angle that works best:** Lifestyle and legacy. LA buyers respond to build a real business, not a side hustle positioning. (Latino market / RE and Mortgage overlap / Lifestyle hooks)

**New York City — June 19, 2026**

- **Geo radius:** Tri-State (NY, NJ, CT). Pull from Philadelphia, Boston, and DC metros as secondary.
- **Cultural notes:** Diverse immigrant entrepreneur base. Compliance and legitimacy hooks resonate strongly in this market.
- **Industry density:** Larger pool of finance, insurance, and tax prep professionals looking to diversify.
- **Angle that works best:** Compliance, professionalism, and do this the right way. NYC buyers value structure and credentials. (Tri-State reach / Compliance angle / Professional positioning)

## 11. Who This Is Not For

Filtering the wrong audience out is as important as attracting the right one. The following groups should be actively excluded from creative and targeting so ticket quality stays high and the backend coaching call shows are qualified.

- Freebie seekers who only attend when events are free
- Consumers looking to repair their own credit, not start a business
- Multi level marketing recruiters looking for a stage to pitch from
- People with zero interest in compliance or systems
- Operators who already run businesses above $50K per month, they need a different offer
