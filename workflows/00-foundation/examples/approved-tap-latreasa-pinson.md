## Section 01. The Woman We Are Speaking To

*She is the woman who pours into everyone. Her kids, her job, her household, her people. She rarely pours back into herself. The Mother's Day Brunch is the permission slip she did not know she was waiting for.*

**Meet Mia. — Primary Persona · 70% of Ticket Buyers**

Mia is 38. She lives in Southfield, West Bloomfield, Novi, or Detroit's west side. Two kids. A partner or a co-parent. A 9-to-5 or her own hustle. Household income $75K to $140K. She buys experiences over things, lives on Instagram and Facebook, and belongs to a church, a sorority, a book club, or all three.

She is the one who plans the family reunion and remembers everyone's anniversary. When she sees a Mother's Day Brunch ad, the first thought is not "Should I go?" It is "Who am I bringing?"

> *"I deserve a moment that feels like mine. And I want my girls in the photo."*

**Demographics**

- **Age:** 32 to 55, sweet spot 36 to 48
- **Gender:** Women
- **Geo:** 25 mile radius of Farmington Hills
- **Income:** $75K to $140K household
- **Status:** Mothers, married or single
- **Education:** Some college to graduate

**Psychographics**

- **Identity:** Mother first, woman second, in that order
- **Values:** Sisterhood, faith, legacy, presentation
- **Energy:** Generous, tired, secretly craves attention
- **Aesthetic:** Elevated. Cute. Photo-ready.
- **Spending:** Will splurge for a feeling, not a thing

---

## Section 02. What Moves Her to Buy

*She does not need convincing that she deserves it. She needs three things: permission, a beautiful reason, and someone to go with.*

1. **Mom Guilt Relief** — "This counts as quality time because I'm a better mom when I'm filled up."
2. **Sisterhood Pull** — She buys two tickets the first time, three the second. Bringing the girls is the experience.
3. **The Feeling of Royalty** — Crowns, pearls, rose gold, a curated table. She wants to feel chosen, not just included.
4. **Instagrammable** — If she cannot post it, she will not feel it landed. The room must photograph.
5. **Limited Seats** — "Only 150 women" reads as exclusive, not corporate. Scarcity that feels like an invitation.
6. **A Promo Code** — SPARKLE10 closes the loop. She loves a small win that says she was smart to act fast.

**Messaging Hooks That Work on Her**

- *She poured into everyone this year. Pour into her on May 9th.*
- *Mother's Day is one day. This is the moment she will remember it by.*
- *Bring your mama. Bring your girls. Bring your daughter. Just don't bring guilt.*
- *150 seats. Pearls, rose gold, and a room full of women who get it.*

## Section 04. Who We Are Not Selling To

Every dollar spent reaching the wrong woman is a seat the right woman did not get to take. Exclude these audiences from ads and messaging:

- ✕ Women under 28 with no maternal connection or buyer intent
- ✕ Audiences outside a 30 mile radius of Farmington Hills
- ✕ Bargain seekers and "Free Mother's Day" event hunters
- ✕ Corporate or wedding event planners shopping for venues
- ✕ Out of market wholesale or vendor accounts

---

## Section 05. The Other Buyers We Will Not Ignore

*Mia is 70 percent of the room. The remaining 30 percent buys for her, with her, or because of her. We market to all three.*

**The Devoted Daughter**
- **Who:** Women age 22 to 40 buying for mom
- **Pull:** Wants to give an experience, not a card
- **Tier:** Skews to the $100 VIP ticket
- **Hook:** "She raised you. Treat her like the queen she is."

**The Thoughtful Partner**
- **Who:** Husbands, boyfriends, co-parents
- **Pull:** Looking for the gift that earns serious points
- **Tier:** Will buy 2 VIP tickets so she brings a friend
- **Hook:** "Skip the brunch reservation. Get her a moment she'll talk about for a year."

**The Group Buyer**
- **Who:** The friend who organizes everything
- **Pull:** Wants the table of 4 to 6 girlfriends
- **Tier:** Mixes General and VIP within one purchase
- **Hook:** "Get the girls. Book the table. Make it a thing."

**The Multi-Generation Buyer**
- **Who:** Mid-life women buying for self, mom, daughter
- **Pull:** Three generations, one table, zero excuses
- **Tier:** Highest lifetime value, highest referral rate
- **Hook:** "Mama. You. Your baby girl. One table. May 9th."
